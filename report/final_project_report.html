Introduction

This project will use publicly available National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES) August 2021–August 2023 data to examine socioeconomic and racial disparities in Hepatitis B serologic immunity among U.S. adults aged 20 years and older. The data contains laboratory results for Hepatitis B surface antibody used to assess vaccine-derived immunity, along with respondent-level demographic and socioeconomic variables (age, sex, race/ethnicity, family income to poverty ratio, and education level).

Analysis Objectives

The final R Markdown report will include a Table 1 summarizing participant characteristics by Hepatitis B immunity. A figure will be presented to illustrate differences in immunity across education levels. In addition, a multivariable logistic regression model will be fitted to estimate adjusted associations between demographic and socioeconomic factors and Hepatitis B immunity, with results presented in a regression table.
